\section{Section 1: Derivation}

\subsection*{Notes to remember}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Sam's original transitivity breaking document and before changing it in his paper had $f(x_j, x_k) = r_k - r_j$, where $$r_j = \alpha' (b_{j-1} - b_j + \bar{h}(\delta_{jq} - \delta_{jp})) $$ and the margin was negative.  This produces $D_{jp} - D_{jq}$. By reversing that definition so $f(x_j, x_k) = r_j - r_k$, we now have $$r_j = \alpha' (b_{j} - b_{j-1} + \bar{h}(\delta_{jp} - \delta_{jq})). $$ This produces $D_{jq} - D_{jp}$. 
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Rank Representation}
